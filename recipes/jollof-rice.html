Jollof Rice

Description

Jollof Rice is a beloved West African dish celebrated for its rich, smoky, and savory flavors. Made by cooking rice in a vibrant tomato and pepper-based sauce, it is seasoned with a blend of aromatic spices and often includes vegetables and proteins like chicken, fish, or shrimp. This one-pot dish is a centerpiece at gatherings and celebrations, uniting people across the region with its bold taste and cultural significance. Perfect as a main dish or a side, Jollof Rice is a flavorful experience that's cherished worldwide.

Ingredients

Tomato sauce:

  • 1 large, ripe tomato, cored and quartered
  • 1 large red bell pepper, seeded and roughly chopped
  • 1 whole habanero pepper, stem removed
  • 1 whole Fresno chili, or jalapeño, stem removed
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1 tablespoon minced ginger
  • 2 cups water

Rice:

  • 1/3 cup olive oil
  • 1 red onion, diced
  • 1 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric
  • 2 1/4 cups basmati rice
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tablespoon chicken bouillon paste
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onion for garnish (optional)
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro, for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Combine tomato, red bell pepper, habanero pepper, Fresno chili, garlic, ginger, and water in a blender for the sauce. Blend on high until smooth and set aside.
  2. Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add onion and salt and sauté until the onion starts to soften, about 4 to 5 minutes. Clear space in the middle of the pan, add tomato paste, and sauté for 2 to 3 minutes.
  3. Season with paprika, curry powder, cumin, thyme, pepper, and turmeric. Continue cooking for 1 to 2 minutes until everything is well combined.
  4. Turn off the heat, and stir in the rice, making sure every grain of rice is coated with the oil-tomato mixture. Add bay leaf and chicken bouillon paste.
  5. Turn heat to high, stir in the tomato sauce, and bring to a vigorous simmer. Cover with a tight-fitting lid, reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer for 20 minutes. Do not remove the lid or stir the rice.
  6. After 20 minutes, turn off the heat and set a timer for 12 minutes. Allow the rice to sit, keeping the lid on.
  7. After 12 minutes, remove the lid and fluff the rice with a fork, separating all the grains. Serve garnished with green onions and chopped cilantro.

Cook's Note:

If rice seems to be a little undercooked at this point, stop fluffing. Pat the rice down gently with the back of the fork, cover tightly again, and set heat to medium low. Cook for another 5 minutes, and check again.
